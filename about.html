About Clairity

Clairity exists to make indoor air quality understandable, measurable, and manageable for every school and small building—not just large institutions with enterprise budgets.

We believe that healthy air is essential for learning, comfort, and performance, and that every building deserves access to clear data, not assumptions.

Who We Are

Clairity was founded in Texas by Milan Chandran, a senior at Allen High School who saw first-hand how inconsistent classroom air quality affected comfort, focus, and performance.

While working across school labs, HVAC spaces, and independent electronics projects, Milan realized a simple truth: Most classrooms and offices have hidden ventilation problems—yet no one has the data to see them.

Instead of accepting that, he built a solution.

Our Approach

    Clairity combines portable sensors, offline-capable data logging, and a clean, visual dashboard that transforms complex air-quality data into simple, actionable insights.

    We focus specifically on environments like:

  • Schools & districts
  • Offices and workspaces
  • Gyms and small facilities
  • Property-managed buildings
  • Clinics and educational environments

    • These are buildings that traditionally struggle to access affordable, room-level IAQ monitoring.

    Clairity changes that.

    Many building technologies are developed by large companies far removed from the realities of classrooms and small facilities.

    Clairity was built from the ground up with:

  • first-hand experience inside classrooms, labs, and HVAC spaces
  • a direct understanding of how staff and students experience the environment
  • a practical, engineering-driven mindset
  • respect for school budgets and operational constraints


    • Instead of developing technology for “ideal conditions,” Clairity was designed for real buildings—tight budgets, aging HVAC, mixed ventilation patterns, and busy administrators.

Built in Texas

Clairity is based in Texas and shaped by the challenges of local schools and small commercial buildings.
Every feature—from offline data logging to portable sensors to simple reporting—was designed to fit the needs of real facilities in the region.

The Story Continues

Clairity is actively partnering with schools, small businesses, and local districts to bring room-level air data to more environments.

As the platform grows, so does our commitment to practical, affordable indoor air intelligence.

— Milan Chandran, Founder of Clairity
