Healthy Air Protects People, Reduces Liability, and Builds Trust

Poor IAQ harms performance
High CO₂ reduces cognitive function by 15–50% (Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, COGfx Study).

Ventilation failures increase illness
Insufficient ventilation significantly increases transmission of respiratory infections (CDC, ASHRAE).

Regulations and expectations are rising
ASHRAE 62.1 recommends maintaining CO₂ near outdoor-equivalent levels during occupancy. Many states now require or encourage IAQ monitoring.

Transparent data strengthens trust
Clear IAQ reporting reduces complaints, reassures staff/parents/tenants, and supports due diligence.

This section illustrates how organizations typically use Clairity to identify, diagnose, and resolve indoor air-quality issues. Each step reflects a real progression administrators, facility managers, and building operators follow when they lack visibility into ventilation performance.

A school or small commercial building begins experiencing recurring reports of “stuffy” or uncomfortable rooms, inconsistent airflow, or reduced alertness among occupants.

Traditional troubleshooting yields limited insight, as there is no objective data to confirm the root cause.

Maintenance teams attempt standard responses—adjusting HVAC schedules, checking vents, replacing filters, or relocating occupants—yet the concerns persist.

Without room-specific measurements, decisions remain based on assumptions rather than evidence.

The organization seeks a more reliable method to assess indoor air quality and schedules a Clairity demonstration.

They learn that portable sensors can provide continuous CO₂, VOC, temperature, and humidity data for each room, even without building Wi-Fi or IT involvement.

Clairity sensors are placed in areas of concern. Setup requires only minutes, and data begins logging immediately.

No installation, drilling, or system integration is necessary.

Within 24–48 hours, the dashboard presents clear ventilation patterns, highlighting rooms with elevated CO₂, trapped air, or abnormal conditions.

The organization gains objective visibility that was previously unavailable.

Using the room-by-room analysis, facility staff identify the true source of the issue—such as insufficient ventilation, airflow imbalance, occupancy loading, or equipment irregularities.

The insights replace guesswork with actionable information.

The building team makes specific adjustments—such as modifying airflow settings, adjusting equipment behavior, or correcting blocked vents.

Clairity then confirms whether these interventions resolved the problem, enabling efficient, evidence-based remediation.

Occupant comfort improves, complaints decline, and the organization gains documented proof of healthier air and compliant ventilation.

Ongoing monitoring helps prevent future issues, supports regulatory expectations, and reinforces transparency with staff, parents, or tenants.
